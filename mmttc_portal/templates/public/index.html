Introduction

Banaras Hindu University

Banaras Hindu University (BHU), a Central University established in 1916 located in Varanasi, India is the largest residential university in Asia. The fully-residential campus has more than 140 independent teaching departments.

Its 1350 acre campus was built on land donated by Kashi Naresh. Total enrollment in the University is over 18,000 including students from India and abroad.

Several of its colleges including IIT-BHU, Faculty of Law, and Institute of Medical Sciences are ranked amongst the best institutions in India.

UGC-Malaviya Mission Teacher Training Centre

National Policy on Education (NPE) 1986 emphasized the crucial relationship between teacher motivation and quality of education.

The policy recognized the need for improving quality of teaching and proposed opportunities for professional and career development of teachers.

The objective was to enhance motivation, skills, methodologies, subject orientation, and inculcate innovative and creative academic values.

Objectives Proposed by UGC

  • Organize orientation programmes in pedagogy, psychology, philosophy, and socio-economic concerns for newly appointed lecturers.
  • Conduct refresher courses for teachers at regular intervals.
  • Organize IT-oriented programmes for new and serving teachers.
  • Encourage participation in seminars, symposia, and workshops.

To achieve these objectives, UGC established Academic Staff Colleges in universities across India.

These centres were later renamed UGC-Human Resource Development Centres in 2015 and are now known as Malaviya Mission Teacher Training Centres.

Reading Material

Reading materials are provided in the form of proceedings, compilations, printed publications, and electronic resources.

Library

The centre maintains a well-equipped library containing approximately 1958 books across multiple disciplines.

Evaluation of Participants

Participants are evaluated through objective tests, seminars, project work, participation, and holistic response.

Grading is done as follows:

  • Grade A : 75% and above
  • Grade B : 60% to less than 75%
  • Grade C : 50% to less than 60%
  • Grade D : Below 50%

Teacher participants receiving Grade D must repeat the programme after one year.

Distribution of Marks

  • Multiple-choice objective tests – 30
  • Seminars / presentations – 15
  • Projects / surveys – 20
  • Participation / micro-teaching – 10
  • Holistic response – 25

Course Coordinator

The MMTTC may appoint one coordinator for refresher courses. The coordinator receives an honorarium as per UGC norms.

In Orientation Programmes, one academic staff member may be appointed as coordinator without additional honorarium.
